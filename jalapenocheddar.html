Jalapeño Cheddar Bread

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 cups + 2 Tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons coarse sea salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon instant or actve-dry yeast
  • 1 jalapeño, sliced (and a few more slices for decoration
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese (and some more to sprinkle on top before baking)
  • 1 and 1/2 cup warm water (about 95°F)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Stir the first 5 ingredients together in a large bowl. Stir in the warm water. The dough will be pretty sticky– don’t be tempted to add more flour– you want a sticky dough. Gently shape into a ball as best you can. Cover tightly with plastic wrap or aluminum foil. Set on the counter at room temperature (honestly any normal room temperature is fine!) and allow to rise for 12-18 hours. The dough will double in size, stick to the sides of the bowl, and have a lot of air bubbles.
  2. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured work surface and, using lightly floured hands, shape into a ball as best you can. Doesn’t have to be perfect. Transfer dough to a large piece of parchment paper, a piece large enough to fit inside your dutch oven and one that is safe under high oven heat. Place the ball of dough + parchment inside a bowl so the dough doesn’t spread out as it rests.
  3. Using a very sharp knife or scissors, gently score an X into the top. Cover dough lightly with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. After resting for 30 minutes, preheat the oven to 450°F. Place your dutch oven with the lid OR heavy duty pot inside to heat up.
  5. Sprinkle some cheddar cheese on top of bread dough and place a few slices of the jalapeño for decoration. Once the oven is done preheating, carefully place the dough into the dutch oven with the parchment paper and cover with the lid.
  6. Bake for 25 minutes with the lid on. Carefully remove the lid and continue baking for 8-10 more minutes until the bread is golden brown. Remove pot from the oven, carefully remove the bread from the pot, and allow to cool on the counter for 30 minutes before breaking/slicing/serving.

Enjoy!
